How to Ask Good Questions at V School

Lesson

Learning Objectives

  • Students will know the specific steps they should take when requesting support from within the program.

How To Ask Questions and Get Support

The VSchool Discord Channels will be the primary resource for asking questions about blockers, curriculum, assignments, projects, concepts, or anything code related. The level channels are a positive, encouraging, non-judgmental, and creative place to ask any questions you may have as you progress through the program.

Who you can expect a response from

  • Students (current students in the same or above levels)
  • TA’s
  • V School Alumni
  • Instructors (discord questions, level assessments, support sessions, etc.)

STEPS TO ASK A QUESTION IN DISCORD

  1. Complete all necessary Problem Solving Steps
    • Our rule of thumb is to problem solve on your own for at least 30 min. but no longer than 1hr before asking for help
  2. Post your question in #web-development-questions
    • making your question visible to everyone increases collaboration. It also allows all those in the channel to help with the problem solving process.
    • Please try and avoid Direct Messaging an Instructor or TA for help (see below)
    • You will never be in the wrong messaging a TA or Instructor when necessary, however it is our policy that all questions should try and first be resolved in the #web-development-questions channel. The benefit here is that many students have similar or the exact same questions and sometimes it helps to see that and possibly work the blockers together or share solutions.
    • What to include in your question:
      1. Assignment Name and problem/task as applicable
        • Good example: “I am working on the Problem Solving Practice assignment, problem #2…..”
      2. A detailed description of the problem or blocker
        • identifying the correct problem is the first and most important step of the problem-solving process and is the most efficient way to attack any blocker you encounter.
        • this is also a SKILL that is learned, practiced, and developed over time. START NOW!
        • what you are specifically trying to achieve with your code?
        • Good example: “I am trying to return a value from this function, but it won’t log to the console”
        • Bad example: “My code won’t work”
      3. Any strategies, concepts, or syntax you have attempted
        • what worked, what didn’t
        • Good example: “I have tried console logging the value inside my function declaration, but it is returning UNDEFINED”
        • Bad example: “nothing works”
      4. Any relevant code
        • In slack you can start your message with three backticks to create a code block
        • typing ``` will create the code block, then you can copy and paste or type your code
        • Another option is to take screenshot or snippet of your code and attach with your question
        • For larger projects/assignments please also include a GitHub link as well
      5. Any error messages / snippets help a lot
        • “I am receiving this error message in the console” … (include snippet or syntax of the error message)
    • At VSchool we encourage support and collaboration from not only the staff but other Students as well. There is a lot of really amazing learning that happens when working through blockers with others. Even if just one other. Meet classmates and work together.
    • Try to avoid vague or generalized questions such as:
      • “Can I get help?”
      • “Is there a TA/Instructor available?”
      • “Can anyone hop on a call real quick”?
      • These types of questions could potentially lead to longer wait times but more importantly robs you of the experience communicating your code and detailing blockers to others. This is a very important skill needed to be successful in many potential careers in tech.
  3. Resolve Question with TA/Instructor/Student
    • once a TA/Instructor is available they will reply directly to your question thread to help problem solve the blocker or question.
    • the problem solving process can be quick or lengthy at times depending on the blocker.
    • for blockers that are not solved during the support session, questions are tagged UNRESOLVED in the question queue and prioritized by the TAs
